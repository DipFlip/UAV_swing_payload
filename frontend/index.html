Control Algorithms

LQR (Linear Quadratic Regulator) — Full-state feedback using all 10 state variables including pendulum angles and angular velocities. Solves the continuous algebraic Riccati equation for optimal gains. Knows about the pendulum dynamics and actively compensates for swing.

PID (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) — Classic 3-axis position control. Only sees drone position error, completely blind to pendulum dynamics. Uses derivative-on-measurement to avoid setpoint kick. Simple but causes more payload swing.

Cascade PD — Two nested control loops. The outer loop measures payload position error and computes a desired drone offset. The inner loop drives the drone to that offset. By explicitly tracking the payload (not just the drone), it naturally reduces swing without needing a pendulum model.

Flatness FF (Differential Flatness + Feedforward) — Exploits the mathematical property that the payload position is a "flat output" of the system. Smooths the goal through a reference filter, then algebraically inverts the dynamics to compute where the drone should be and what force to apply. Combines feedforward with PD feedback on both drone position and pendulum angle.

Anti-Swing Damping

Energy-based swing damping that can be layered on any algorithm. Adds a force proportional to the pendulum's angular velocity (F = k · phi_dot) that actively dissipates swing energy. The force "chases" the swing direction, which shortens the effective pendulum and damps oscillations.

Aggression

Scales controller responsiveness using quadratic scaling. At low aggression, controllers are gentle and slow. At high aggression, position tracking weights increase quadratically while control cost decreases, making the drones respond much more aggressively to position errors.
